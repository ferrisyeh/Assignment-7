Hey there,

Welcome to my top five picks for portfolios that I think are professional, sleek, and worthy of being used as references when building a portfolio. There were a few portfolios that I would have loved to put into this assignment (like this one and this one) but while I loved the aesthetics of those sites, I felt that the portfolio's contents were not quite what I could use as an example for my own portfolio. I picked these five as a result of the following factors:


  • Skill set
  • Professionalism
  • Similar career paths
  • Overall aesthetic

To jump to each portfolio that I talked about, click on the links in the navigation bar to get to each respective portfolio. You'll notice that these portfolios have many projects based in UI/UX, visual design, or graphic design, and are similar in aesthetic!


So without further ado, here are my top five picks! ↓

1. Kenny Nieh

Kenny’s portfolio is a flat design that is very simple, very straightforward, and very professional. Each of the projects are clear examples of his skillset – they document his role in each project, the process, and the final product. Furthermore, Kenny includes a PDF of his resume which also outline his skills as a UI/UX designer on his About page. The color scheme is monochromatic and clean, with pops of muted blue to accentuate and highlight his portfolio. He uses Sans-serif fonts, specifically Open Sans and Proxima Nova, which further add to the professional image.

Return to top ↑

2. Elizabeth Kang

Elizabeth’s portfolio, like Kenny’s, is starkly minimalistic and again, is a flat design portfolio. Each of her projects demonstrate her skillset as well, documenting her role and walking the viewer through her process. The color scheme is monochromatic, with a few pops of color that relate to each of her projects, although notably most of the colors used are cool colors. She uses sans-serif fonts as well, specifically Futura Pt and Proxima Nova, which also contribute to her portfolio’s professional and minimalistic image.

Return to top ↑

3. Jennet Liaw

If not for the bright and vivid snapshots of each of her projects, Jennet’s portfolio would be as monochromatic as Elizabeth’s and Kenny’s. However, despite the loud and eye-catching photos that serve as links to each of her projects, Jennet’s portfolio, too, is simple and utilizes a flat design. She uses sans-serif fonts, specifically Brandon Grotesque and Futura Pt, but takes it one step further by bolding and italicizing the fonts. Jennet’s portfolio is both professional and fun.

Return to top ↑

4. Dawn Lee

A stylistic combination of Jennet’s, Elizabeth’s, and Kenny’s portfolios, Dawn’s portfolio is a flat-design website that is mostly black-and-white with pops of pink and yellow to make her logo and links stand out. Dawn captions each of her projects with tags that tells the viewer what each project was about. Like the other portfolios above, Dawn uses sans-serif fonts, giving her portfolio a clean and professional image.

Return to top ↑

5. Ishaan Kansal

Ishaan’s portfolio is definitely a little more interactive than the other ones. It is also a flat-design website that is monochromatic and decorated with pops of color that relate to each project. The font that is used is Raleway, which is also sans-serif, giving the website a beautiful professional image. Ishaan’s skillset is exemplified through each project, and his specialties are summarized with the captions on each of his projects. It is a minimalistic and professional looking portfolio.
